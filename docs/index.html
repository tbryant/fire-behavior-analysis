About This Analysis

This fire behavior analysis system uses real LANDFIRE data and implements the Rothermel surface fire spread model to predict wildfire behavior under extreme conditions.

The comprehensive fire map simulates a worst-case scenario based on the 2017 Tubbs Fire, which burned over 36,000 acres in Sonoma County. The simulation uses:

  • Wind: 70 mph from the northeast (45Â°)
  • Fuel Moisture: 2% (critically dry)
  • Temperature: 95Â°F with 25% relative humidity
  • Ignition Point: Northeast grasslands (continuous fuel)
  • Duration: 48-hour projection

The visualization includes toggleable layers for fuel models (FBFM40), slope analysis, time-to-arrival isochrones, and satellite imagery for terrain context.

Data Sources: LANDFIRE (U.S. Department of the Interior) | Methods: Rothermel (1972), Scott & Burgan (2005) | Simulation: Cellular wave propagation with Huygens' principle
